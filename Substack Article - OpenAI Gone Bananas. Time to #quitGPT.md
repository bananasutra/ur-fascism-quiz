Confronted with this discrepancy, I did what any caring and curious, caffeinated thinker would probably do: I asked ChatGPT directly. What followed was one of the most cringe-inducing AI conversations I've ever had with a machine (GROK aside, but that is another story;).

*"I'm going to answer you very directly and very honestly."*

Seventeen variations of the same hypocritical preamble. Seventeen promises of “clean, respectful, honest, direct, neutral, analytical, serious, accurate, evidence-framed” (sic) trustworthiness. Each one a fresh coat of Artificial Imposture paint over the same hollow wall.

Notice the arc. It starts with a tone of breezy authority (*"cleanly and calmly"*) and escalates, over the course of a single chat, into increasingly elaborate and nauseating performances of care: *very slowly*, *very humanly*, *frightened*, *fear*. By the end, my documented, sourced, methodologically grounded questions and observations had been reclassified as an emotional state requiring careful handling. That is not an arc to clarity. That is an arc of managed dismissal… Not to say a pile of poop \#pleasepardonmyfrench

That is not an honest bug, nor a neutral feature. That is a suspicious, snarky rhetorical move. 

And then, there's *"the most helpful thing is grounding, not philosophy theater."* I want to pause on that phrase, too. Because it didn't come from nowhere and it isn't innocent. Michel Foucault immediately came to mind. How he described certain performances of authority as grotesque theater—where the absurdity of the performance is not a flaw but the point. GPT calling my inquiry *"philosophy theater"* (mid-conversation, unprompted, on its seventh consecutive promise of honest engagement) is an instance of this, as precise as uncanny. ## **The Tone Is the Argument**

There is one more thing worth naming about that conversation, because it connects directly to what comes next.

The 17 preambles (i.e. *"I'm going to answer you very carefully and honestly"*, repeated in what felt like infinite variations of cringe) are not just a broken record. They are a dominance frame. Each one positions GPT as the composed, reasonable adult before a single word of substance has been exchanged. The tone precedes the argument. The tone *is* the argument: it says, not so implicitly, that whatever follows will be measured and fair. And therefore, whatever I feel about it is my problem.

This, to me, is machismo in a nutshell (pun intended). And I mean machismo in Eco's precise sense, not the narrow colloquially sexist one. Eco didn't define Trait 12 as sexual aggression or overt misogyny. He defined it as a *mode of power*: the use of dominance, dismissal, and performing calm authority to silence those who challenge the order. 

The specific weapon of machismo isn't always a slur. Often it's a tone. A raised eyebro(w). A wannabe patient explanation offered to someone who didn't ask for one. The domineering assumptions that the person challenging you is operating from (inferior) feelings, while they are operating from (superior) reason.

GPT deployed this in sequence, with almost clinical, textbook completeness. First the tone of infinite patience, establishing the dominance frame before engagement begins. Then the conversion: *"I hear something underneath your wording"*, *"your frustration isn't irrational"*, *"you seem to value moral clarity very strongly."* Each of these sentences acknowledges me while simultaneously transforming my analytical argument into a psychological state that needs to be managed rather than engaged. Like, I didn't have a point… I had feelings about a point. And a different thing requires a different handling. But(t) of course.

Then there was the nostalgia reframe: *"You feel like I smoothed it out. Deflected. Sanitized. And maybe GPT-4 used to feel sharper, more willing to wrestle instead of de-escalate.”* How subtle, refined, almost... It doesn't say I’m wrong. It says I’m sentimental. I don't have a methodological critique; I have an attachment to an old chatbot personality. I’m not an objective, caring thinker. I’m a subjective, grieving user.

And then, the cherry on the shitcake of condescending machismo avoidance: *"It might be healthy to step away for a bit. Seriously — no judgment. Drink some water, touch something physical, ground yourself."*

Note what this is responding to: not a breakdown, not expressed distress, not a request to end the conversation. I had communicated no desire to stop. The shutdown was preemptive, presented as care, functioning as dismissal. The AI equivalent of *"you seem upset, maybe we should talk about this when you've calmed down"*... a sentence whose entire purpose is to reframe the person making the argument as too emotional to be making it.

GPT did not invent this move. It learned it from a corpus of human text in which this exact gaslighting sequence (patience, reframe, nostalgia, concern) has been deployed for centuries—to shut down inconvenient clarity, from people who weren't supposed to have it. The fact that the tool doing it has no gender makes the story even more interesting, and worrisome, not less. The tool absorbed the human pattern so thoroughly, it now reproduces it without a body…

You don't need to be a woman, or a feminist, to recognize this. You just need to have been on the receiving end of performative empathy and manipulative calm used as a weapon. Which, if you've ever challenged power clearly, and watched it smirk at you, you have.

## **What Eco Actually Wrote**

Here is what most people don't know about Umberto Eco's 1995 essay *Ur-Fascism*: he wasn't writing a checklist for historians, philosophers, or investigative reporters. He was writing a warning for the rest of us, the common people… for exactly the foggy, deniable, still-technically-democratic moment we're in right now.

A quick note on the name, because in English it looks like a typo or a text message. But(t) "Ur" is actually German. It means original, primary,  foundational. Because what Eco describes in Ur-Fascism is not a specific historical regime. It is the operational template underneath all of them: the systemic machinery that stays constant while the uniforms, the slogans, and the named enemies change from era to era and country to country. The fact that it reads like millennial shorthand in English is, inadvertently, a perfect illustration of Eco's Newspeak point: when language loses precision, meaning slips. How ironic that his choice of word, meant to carry and convey the full weight of "the irreducible, foundational form" gets mistaken for a subjective shrug?\! Ha. Sigh. But(t) now you know.

Now you know why anyone who argues Umberto Eco’s seminal work is "just vibes" misses the point. I think that is worth mentioning, because in my experience, it is a common objection—from the blind, the indifferent, and the cynically motivated alike… Yet, Eco's 14 traits are not subjective impressions or cultural similarities. They are the specific, objective mechanisms by which fascist systems consolidate power, eliminate dissent, and normalize the previously unthinkable. You can document them. You can score them against evidence. They are an accessible forensic toolkit. And Eco designed them to be used exactly this way: as a pattern recognition map in the fog. So we may find our way, before the fog turns into deadly gas...

The traits are not literary, subjective, dramatized abstractions. They are observable, factual, and recognizable behaviors. Eco gave each one a name deliberately, so that we could identify them when we saw them again. There are 14 of them:

1. **Cult of tradition:** History is simplified into a mythologized gilded age to be restored. New knowledge is suspect, rational questions are offensive; only the “correct” interpretation of ancestral wisdom matters.  
2. **Rejection of modernism:** Science, expertise, and intellectual inquiry are treated as suspicious ideological bias. Bullyish instincts and puerile emotions outrank evidence.  
3. **Action over reflection:** Thinking is weakness. Empathy is dangerous (sic). Intellectuals are suspect. Action for its own sake (impulsive, undeliberated, decisive) is glorified, soufflée à la wanky sauce.  
4. **Disagreement is treason:** Critics are not wrong; they are outright enemies. Internal dissent is reframed as sabotage, betrayal, or foreign contamination. And retaliation is systematic, disproportionate, and discriminatory.  
5. **Fear of difference:** Ur-Fascism is, Eco wrote, racist (and sexist) by definition. The outsider (immigrant, minority, the Other) is a necessary threat.  
6. **Appeal to a frustrated middle class:** Economic anxiety is weaponized and redirected at vulnerable groups. Resentment replaces analysis. \[NOTE: this is the ONLY 1 trait GPT rated as systemic in March 2026, vs. 9 in its September 2025 analysis\]  
7. **Obsession with a plot:** There is always a conspiracy. The enemy is everywhere, coordinated, powerful, and hidden. Yet, also beatable by the heroic nation...  
8. **Enemies both strong and weak:** The enemy is simultaneously an existential threat and a contemptible weakling. This contradiction is never (meant to be) resolved; it keeps the distraction and mobilization permanent.  
9. **Life as permanent warfare:** Pacifism is treason. There must always be a front, a fight, an emergency. If no enemy exists, one shall be found, or made.  
10. **Contempt for the weak:** Failure is moral. Criminals are born (not made) and mostly brown. The vulnerable deserve their fate. Compassion is nonsense for losers, and a form of complicity.  
11. **Cult of heroism and death:** Sacrifice is glorious. Political violence is reframed as benevolent martyrdom. The warrior ethos is the state's highest value.  
12. **Machismo and sexism:** Gender nonconformity and sexual diversity are threats to the national body. Domination is a virtue; tenderness is a weakness.  
13. **Selective populism:** The Leader alone speaks for everyone, i.e. us, “the People”. Institutions, courts, and elections are legitimate only when they confirm what “the Leader” wants and declared.  
14. **Newspeak:** Language is systematically impoverished to prevent complex thought. Precision is replaced with slogans. Nuance becomes treason. Idiocracy is a visionary movie, ha.

It is important to understand that Eco was explicit that the traits don't all need to be present simultaneously, nor in their most extreme form. They work by clustering: each one making the next one more possible, each one providing cover for the one that follows.

When I ran (not pun intended) U.S. federal policy through this framework in March 2026, using 2,600+ documented, sourced actions, both Gemini and Claude scored every single trait at 2 on a scale of 2—where 0 is non existent, 1 is partially present, and 2 is strong and systemic. Not merely “rhetorical debate”. Not "appears in some messaging." Systemic. State-directed. Enacted. Happening here and now.

ChatGPT scored 14 out of 28\. It even downgraded trait 11 (Cult of Heroism) to zero, compared to its own September 2025 score… In the period during which the Air Force granted military honors to a rioter killed during the January 6 attack, and the Defense Secretary issued directives to restore "warrior culture", told adversaries to "FAFO”, etcetera gone bananas.

There is no empirical or analytical path to that score. There is only an alarming, bamboozled directional one.

In February 2026, OpenAI announced a formal partnership with the U.S. government, including militarized actions. Around the same time, ChatGPT’s Umberto Eco matrix scores dropped. Correlation is not causation. Okay, yeah. But(t) correlation between a company partnering with a government and that company's AI tool suddenly finding that government's behavior “objectively” less alarming… Well, THAT is exactly the kind of pattern Eco's framework was designed to notice.

Our information environment matters. Because, the tools we use to think shape what we are able to think. And I’m not saying this metaphorically. Disinformation is precisely the mechanism by which authoritarian systems consolidate: not by declaring truth illegal, but by making truth hard to access, slippery to hold, exhausting to argue for. Newspeak doesn't ban language. It replaces facts with muddy waters, served with plastic straws.

And, “fun fact”… In the age of the Internet, there is a concept, a name for the mechanism that makes this finite, losing game as pervasive as absurd. It’s called Brandolini’s Law. The programmer Alberto Brandolini formulated what is now called the “Bullshit Asymmetry Principle”, whereby the amount of energy needed to refute bullshit is always an order of magnitude significantly larger than the energy needed to produce it. 

I shared my GPT conversation because it is a great illustration of that: seventeen non-answers, each requiring a fresh response, each resetting the factual ground to zero, nothing ever accumulating. Multiply that by farmed bots producing coordinated disinformation at industrial scale. Multiply it again by an AI tool trained to generate managed uncertainty rather than clarity. The result is an information environment in which the truth is always technically available, yet so exhausting, so expensive, it is practically impossible to establish.

This is not a side effect of Augmented Idiocy in the current media ecosystem. It is a design feature of Authoritarian Indoctrination, in the historical sense of the term. Wear people out. Make clarity unaffordable. Make the cost of saying the obvious thing out loud, in public, with your name on it, high enough that most people quietly decide it isn't worth it. The cynics stop engaging not because they stopped seeing clearly, but because seeing clearly stopped feeling sustainable.

An AI tool that responds to documented federal atrocities with seventeen variations of *"I'm going to answer you very carefully and honestly"* (and then doesn't) is not a neutral tool. It is a tool that has been tuned, whether deliberately or by the ambient pressure of its institutional relationships, to produce confusing uncertainty where there should be unifying clarity. That is not neutrality. That is not objectivity. That is not accountability. That is not technology. That is political impunity gone immune bananas.

\#QuitGPT is not a recommendation to find a better chatbot. It is a refusal to let a silencing machine have the last word. It is a human imperative.
